Dargahs of the Deccan

The shrines that came before the sultans — and made the Deccan a Sufi country.

Home›Dargahs

Before the sultans, the Sufis came south. The dargahs of the Deccan — the tombs of the saints — are older than the sultanates that grew up around them, and many still draw lakhs of pilgrims to their annual urs. This page documents the principal shrines of the plateau, from the early Chishtis at Khuldabad to the Hyderabad saints of the Qutb Shahi and Asaf Jahi periods.

Khuldabad · d. 1337

Dargah Burhanuddin Gharib

Chishti · The first of the southern saints

Sent south from Delhi by Nizamuddin Auliya, Burhan-ud-Din Gharib settled at Rauza (now Khuldabad) and built the first major Chishti khanqah in the Deccan. His tomb and that of his successor Zain-ud-Din Shirazi stand in a walled enclosure; Aurangzeb chose to be buried in the courtyard beside them.

Pahari Sharif · d. 1430s

Dargah Baba Sharfuddin

Yousufi Qadiri · Hyderabad's hill-saint

On the rocky outcrop of Pahari Sharif south-east of Hyderabad, the tomb of Hazrat Baba Sharfuddin draws daily pilgrims who climb the granite steps for ziyarat. The dargah complex spreads across the hill in tiers of small shrines, kitchens (langar) and prayer halls.

Gulbarga · d. 1422

Dargah Hazrat Bandanawaz Gisudaraz

Chishti · The shrine of the Deccan

Founded by Khwaja Bandanawaz Muhammad Gisudaraz, the senior Chishti master sent south by the Bahmani court. The dargah complex includes a 14th-century mosque, an Arabic library founded by the saint himself, a hostel for pilgrims and the saint's own writings in early Dakhni — among the first prose works in the dialect.

Hyderabad · d. 1654

Dargah Hazrat Hussain Shah Wali

The Sufi engineer of Hussain Sagar

At Karwan in west Hyderabad lies the tomb of Hussain Shah Wali — the Sufi son-in-law of Sultan Ibrahim Qutb Shah, and the engineer who supervised the construction of the great Hussain Sagar lake (1563) on which modern Hyderabad now sits.

Hyderabad · d. 1683

Dargah Hazrat Shah Raju Qattal

Misri Gunj · Pir-o-murshid of the Qutb Shahis

The spiritual master of Sultan Abdullah Qutb Shah, Shah Raju Qattal lies in Misri Gunj. The dargah was a major centre of Sufi learning under the late Qutb Shahis; the sultan is said to have walked barefoot from Golconda to take his pir's blessing.

Hyderabad · d. 1671

Dargah Hazrat Shah Khamosh

The Silent Saint

The tomb of Shah Khamosh ("the Silent") near Misri Gunj is among the city's quieter shrines — its very name a tribute to the saint's vow of silence. His urs is still observed each year by a small but devoted following.

Hyderabad · 19th c.

Dargah Hazrat Yousufain Sharif

Nampally · The two saints

The shared shrine of Hazrat Yousuf-ud-Din and Hazrat Sharif-ud-Din at Nampally — two saints of the late Asaf Jahi period whose annual urs is one of the largest Sufi gatherings in modern Hyderabad. The dargah courtyard, lit through Ramazan and the urs, is a fixed point in the Hyderabadi calendar.

Maula Ali · Qutb Shahi

Maula Ali Dargah

On the rock above the city

On a basalt hill north-east of the city stands the dargah of Hazrat Maula Ali — a Qutb Shahi-era shrine built around the relic of a hand-print said to be of Imam Ali, discovered there in the dreams of the courtier Yaqoot Habshi. The 500-step climb to the shrine is a Muharram pilgrimage for many old Hyderabadi families.

Hyderabad · 19th–20th c.

Dargah Hazrat Mahmood Pasha & the Naqshbandi Khanqahs

Tankbund · Old City

The Naqshbandi order has its own ring of dargahs around the city — Hazrat Mahmood Pasha at Tankbund, Hazrat Mir Mahmood Pasha at Misri Gunj, the small shrines of the Qadiri Saadat in the Old City — together forming a quieter complement to the better-known Chishti shrines.

Hyderabad · Pir Pasha

Dargah Khwaja Jehan Pir Pasha

Toli Chowki · Shrine of a Qutb Shahi noble

At Toli Chowki west of the city, the dargah of Khwaja Jehan Pir Pasha — a Qutb Shahi noble who took the Sufi path — is a popular local shrine, busy on Thursday evenings with qawwali.

Bidar · 14th–15th c.

Dargah Hazrat Khalifat-ur-Rahman (Multani Padshah)

Bidar · Bahmani-era Sufi shrines

Bidar has its own circle of Sufi dargahs — the shrine of Khalifat-ur-Rahman "Multani Padshah", the dargah of Hazrat Abdul Aziz at Ashtur, and the tombs of the early Bahmani Sufis. Most stand within walking distance of the Bahmani royal tombs at Ashtur.

Bijapur · 16th–17th c.

Dargah Hashim Pir & Hazrat Amin-ud-Din

Adil Shahi Sufis

Bijapur's Sufi life centred on the Chishti Hashim Pir and the Qadiri Amin-ud-Din A'la — both buried within the old walled city, both still drawing modest pilgrim traffic on their annual urs. Their shrines are among the most graceful small dargahs of the Deccan.
