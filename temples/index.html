Chalukya · Kakatiya · Vijayanagara · Qutb Shahi-era

Temples of the Deccan

The pre-sultanate stone — Chalukyan caves, Kakatiya star-plans, Vijayanagara complexes and the Telugu shrines under Qutb Shahi protection.

Home›Temples

The Deccan was a great temple country long before the sultans came south. The Chalukyas built at Alampur and Mahanandi, the Kakatiyas at Warangal and Palampet, the Vijayanagara at Hampi and Lepakshi. After 1518 the Qutb Shahis took most of these shrines under royal protection, granting jagirs for their maintenance — a Telugu temple-state nestled inside a Persian sultanate. This page records the principal historic temples of the plateau.

Alampur · 7th–8th c.

Jogulamba & Navabrahma Temples

Badami Chalukya · One of 18 Shakti Peethas

The nine "Navabrahma" sandstone temples at Alampur on the Tungabhadra are the finest surviving group from the Badami Chalukya period. Among them stands the temple of Jogulamba, one of the eighteen Maha Shakti Peethas — a pre-medieval shrine resettled in the 1990s after the Srisailam reservoir submerged its original site.

Srisailam · 12th c.

Mallikarjuna Swamy, Srisailam

One of the twelve Jyotirlingas

On the southern bank of the Krishna, the Mallikarjuna temple at Srisailam is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Shiva and one of the eighteen Shakti Peethas in a single complex. Patronised by the Reddi kings, the Vijayanagara emperors and later the Nizams, who continued the temple's land grants under Asaf Jahi rule.

Mahanandi · 7th c. onwards

Mahanandi Temple

Badami Chalukya · Spring-fed shrines

At Mahanandi at the foot of the Nallamala hills, the Mahanandiswara temple draws water from a perennial spring that fills three rectangular tanks. Its Chalukya core is wrapped in later Vijayanagara additions, and the surrounding "Nava-Nandi" ring of nine Shiva temples makes it one of the more complete pilgrim landscapes in the southern Deccan.

Hanamkonda · 1163

Thousand Pillar Temple

Kakatiya · Star-plan triple-shrine

Built by King Rudra of the Kakatiyas on a star-shaped triple plinth, with separate sanctums for Shiva, Vishnu and Surya. Its monolithic basalt Nandi and its 1,000 carved pillars are emblematic of the Kakatiya style at its height.

Palampet · 1213

Ramappa (Rudreshwara) Temple

Kakatiya · UNESCO World Heritage

Named after the sculptor Ramappa — the only major Indian temple known to be named after its maker. Built by general Recharla Rudra on a sandbox foundation that floats the entire structure, with twelve dancing-madanika bracket figures of extraordinary delicacy. UNESCO inscribed in 2021.

Inavolu · Kakatiya

Inavolu Mallikarjuna Swamy Temple

Kakatiya · The local Shiva

A Kakatiya-era Mallikarjuna temple at Inavolu near Warangal — local Shiva, simple plan, but with a remarkable carved torana that anticipates the great Warangal gateways.

Karmanghat · Kakatiya

Karmanghat Hanuman Temple

South Hyderabad · Kakatiya stonework

The Hanuman temple at Karmanghat in south Hyderabad is held by tradition to have been built by a Kakatiya king who saw the deity in a dream while hunting. The shrine survived the Qutb Shahi conquest with royal protection and is still a major Telugu pilgrim site within the modern city.

Keesaragutta · 5th c. (legendary)

Sri Ramalingeswara Swamy Temple

Keesaragutta hill · Lingas of Rama

On the Keesaragutta hill north-east of Hyderabad, the Ramalingeswara temple is associated by tradition with Lord Rama's installation of a lingam. The site holds Chalukya and Vishnukundin-era ruins, and remains an active pilgrim hill.

Bhadrachalam · 1674

Sri Sita Ramachandra Swamy Temple

Qutb Shahi protection · The Rama of the Godavari

Built by Kancherla Gopanna ("Bhadrachala Ramadas") while serving as a Qutb Shahi tehsildar — funded, controversially, from state revenue. Tradition holds that Sultan Abu'l Hasan Qutb Shah eventually repaid the diverted funds himself after Rama and Lakshmana appeared to him with the money in a dream.

Yadagirigutta · medieval

Sri Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy Temple

Yadadri · The Narasimha of Telangana

On the Yadagirigutta hill, the cave-temple of Lakshmi Narasimha Swamy has been a major Vaishnava pilgrim shrine since the medieval period. The temple was rebuilt under Telangana state patronage in 2016–2022 in a vast new black-granite complex around the original cave.

Chilkur · medieval

Chilkur Balaji Temple

Visa Balaji · The 108-pradakshina temple

On the bank of the Osman Sagar west of Hyderabad, the small Chilkur Balaji temple has become the city's most-visited Vaishnava shrine — known popularly as "Visa Balaji" for the eleven prayer-circuits its devotees walk before a foreign-visa interview, and the 108 they walk after a successful one.

Vemulawada · medieval

Sri Raja Rajeshwara Swamy Temple

Western Chalukya · Dakshina Kashi

At Vemulawada north of Karimnagar, the Raja Rajeshwara temple is one of the great Shaiva pilgrim centres of Telangana — often called "Dakshina Kashi". Its origins lie in the Vemulawada Chalukya dynasty of the 8th century.

Komuravelli · medieval

Komuravelli Mallanna Temple

Folk Shaiva · The shepherd's god

The Mallanna shrine at Komuravelli — a regional form of Shiva worshipped by the Yadava and shepherd communities of Telangana — draws enormous Sunday and full-moon pilgrim crowds, with its great Bonalu and Patnam festivals each spring.

Lepakshi · 1538

Veerabhadra Temple, Lepakshi

Vijayanagara · Hanging pillar & mural ceilings

On the southern edge of the old Vijayanagara territories, the Veerabhadra temple at Lepakshi is famed for its hanging pillar, its monolithic Nandi (the largest in India) and a ceiling painted with the full Mahabharata and Ramayana — the most extensive surviving Vijayanagara mural cycle.

Hampi · 14th–16th c.

Virupaksha & Vittala Temple Complexes

Vijayanagara · UNESCO World Heritage

At Hampi on the Tungabhadra, the Virupaksha temple has been continuously worshipped since the 7th century, while the great Vittala temple — with its stone chariot and musical pillars — is the iconic monument of Vijayanagara art. Together with the bazaar at Hampi they form the heart of the UNESCO inscription.

Pillalamarri · 12th c.

Chennakesava & Erakeshwara Temples

Kakatiya · Banyan-shaded triple-shrine

Beneath the great banyan of Pillalamarri near Suryapet stand three Kakatiya-period temples — Chennakesava, Erakeshwara and Namesweswara — small but beautifully carved, and among the few intact ensembles of the period in Telangana.
